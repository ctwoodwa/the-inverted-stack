# UPF — Ch15 (Security Architecture) split

## Verdict (top of file for the impatient)

**Split Ch15 into:**

1. **Ch15 — Security Architecture** (Part III, slimmed): keeps the architectural primitives. Target ~5,000 words.
2. **Ch22 — Security Operations** (NEW; placed in Part V alongside Ch21 Fleet Management): absorbs the operational security material. Target ~10,000 words after PAO's editorial prune.

Net effect: Part V grows from 1 chapter to 2 (Ch21 Fleet + Ch22 Security Ops), giving Part V real structural weight; Ch15 returns to the architectural-spec lane Part III is supposed to occupy; Ch16 (Persistence) stays Ch16 — zero renumber in Parts IV+. This is the AHA: Part V's "Operational Concerns" framing was the natural home for security-ops the whole time; the snapshot's recommendation of "Ch15a or Ch16-equivalent" missed it because Part V wasn't in `book-structure.md` yet.

If after PAO's prune Ch22 lands above 12,000 words, split into **Ch22 — Key Lifecycle Operations** (KCIR + Key-Loss Recovery + Forward Secrecy) + **Ch23 — Endpoint + Collaborator Operations** (Offline Revocation + Collaborator Revocation + Endpoint Compromise). That's the kill-trigger.

## Stage 0 — Discovery & Sparring

### Existing work + feasibility

- Ch15 is currently **22,274 words** (5.5× target). 11 top-level sections. The section seams are clean — the chapter has a clear architectural-spec block (Threat Model + Four Defensive Layers + Key Hierarchy + Role Attestation + In-Memory Key Handling, ~6,500 words pre-prune) and a clear operational block (Key Compromise IR + Key-Loss Recovery + Offline Revocation + Collaborator Revocation + Forward Secrecy + Endpoint Compromise, ~14,000 words pre-prune). No section straddles both modes.
- **Part V exists in `chapters/`** (Ch21 Fleet Management was added by extension #11) but **not in `book-structure.md`**. CO already approved adding Part V to the structure doc in this same PR. That makes Part V a real placement option for new chapters.
- Extension #47 (endpoint-compromise) is **staged-but-unapplied** in Ch15. PAO's snapshot flags this as a merge-conflict risk. Resolution path: extension #47 lands FIRST, then the split executes against the post-#47 chapter. The split absorbs #47's content into Ch22 anyway.

### AHA Effect (the simpler approach the original recommendation missed)

PAO's snapshot recommended "Ch15a or Ch16-equivalent" — both options stay inside Part III. **Better:** move operational security to Part V. Reasons:

1. **Part V's framing is "Operational Concerns" by definition** (Ch21 fleet management). Security operations IS an operational concern. Ch21 alone makes Part V feel like a one-chapter Part — odd in a book that has 4-6 chapters elsewhere. Adding security-ops gives Part V coherence.
2. **No Part IV / Part III renumbering.** Ch16 stays Ch16; Ch17–Ch20 unchanged; appendix letters unchanged. Zero cross-reference cascade. The only cross-reference work is "this chapter's operational content moved to Ch22" (forward reference inside Ch15 + back reference inside Ch22).
3. **Reader narrative is cleaner.** Part III specifies the architecture. Part IV implements it. Part V operates it. Security follows the same arc: Ch15 specifies the architecture; the operational security flows belong in the "operate it" part. Putting them mid-Part-III interrupts the architecture-specification thread.

### Better alternatives (considered + rejected)

| Option | Why rejected |
|---|---|
| **Ch15a sub-numbering** | Non-standard for technical books; signals "we couldn't decide where this goes"; readers expect either Chapter N or Appendix X. |
| **Ch16-renumber** (security ops becomes Ch16, persistence shifts to Ch17, cascades) | File renames + manifest updates + cross-references in Part IV chapters + voice-plan.yaml + every "we'll cover this in Ch20" → "Ch21" rewrite. Substantial mechanical churn for no narrative gain. |
| **Aggressive prune Ch15 to ~10k single chapter** | Doesn't fix the structural problem — a single 10k-word security chapter is still too dense; PAO already flagged this is structural, not just length. |
| **Move security-ops into Part IV (implementation playbooks)** | Part IV is at 304% bloat already (Ch20 alone). Adding ~10k words makes it worse. Also: operational-security content is "running it," not "building it" — wrong Part. |
| **Move security-ops into an appendix** | Appendices are reference material (lookup tables, citation lists). The operational content has narrative flow (recovery scenarios, runbooks). Wrong genre. |
| **Don't split — keep Ch15 at 22k** | Reader-experience regression. Already PAO's flagged judgment; not contested. |

## Stage 1 — The Plan

### 1. Context & Why

Ch15 at 22,274 words is a structural problem (5.5× target; mixes architectural-spec content with operational-security flows). Reader-experience cost is high (Part III's narrative thread breaks; the chapter reads as "everything dumped into security"). The split restores Part III to architectural-specification mode and gives Part V a natural second chapter. Total word count drops from 22,274 to ~15,000 across the two chapters after PAO's prune — recovers ~7,000 words toward the +71%-over-target deficit.

### 2. Success Criteria

- **Ch15 (slimmed) ≤ 5,500 words.** Architectural primitives only: Threat Model, Four Defensive Layers, Key Hierarchy, Role Attestation Flow, In-Memory Key Handling. Forward-references to Ch22 for operational flows.
- **Ch22 (new) ≤ 12,000 words after prune** (target 10k). Operational content: Key Compromise IR, Key-Loss Recovery, Offline Revocation, Collaborator Revocation, Forward Secrecy, Endpoint Compromise.
- **Net word-count reduction ≥ 5,000 words** vs. the original 22,274. (PAO's prune recovers material that was redundant or tangential, not just relocated.)
- **Zero broken cross-references** in the build (`make lint` passes).
- **Both chapters individually readable** without sequential dependency. Reader can land on Ch22 from a search hit and not need to read Ch15 first to understand the recovery flow.
- **Part V book-structure.md entry exists** with both Ch21 and Ch22 listed.

### FAILED conditions (kill triggers)

- **Ch22 lands above 12,000 words after prune** → split into Ch22 (Key Lifecycle Ops: KCIR + Key-Loss Recovery + Forward Secrecy) + Ch23 (Endpoint + Collaborator Ops: Offline Revocation + Collaborator Revocation + Endpoint Compromise). Re-run Phase 4+ for Ch23.
- **Architectural seam fails** (a section straddles spec/ops modes and can't cleanly bisect) → escalate to CO; section may need rewriting before split, not just relocation.
- **Build lint fails on cross-references after Phase 5** that PAO can't mechanically fix → escalate; manual narrative rewrites needed.
- **Reader-experience regression on PAO's read of new Ch15 + Ch22** → revert; rethink boundary.
- **Extension #47 conflict surfaces** that the staged-then-split sequence can't resolve → halt; PAO writes pao-question-* beacon to XO.

### 3. Assumptions & Validation

| Assumption | Validate by | Impact if wrong |
|---|---|---|

| Cross-references from other chapters can be mechanically updated | grep `chapters/` for `Ch.?15`, `Chapter 15`, `chapter 15` (case-insensitive); inventory results | If >5 matches need narrative-context rewrite (not just label swap): manual review per ref; +2–3h. |
| Extension #47 lands cleanly before the split | Apply #47 in its own PR per the existing extension flow; verify the citation renumber didn't introduce new issues. | If #47 itself stalls: split sequence pauses on Phase 0. |
